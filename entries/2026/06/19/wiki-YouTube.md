## YouTube: Platform Overview and History

YouTube is an American online video-sharing platform owned by Google (Alphabet Inc.). Founded in February 2005 by former PayPal employees Chad Hurley, Steve Chen, and Jawed Karim, it was acquired by Google in November 2006 for $1.65 billion. It is the second-most-visited website globally, with 2.7 billion monthly active users as of January 2024, over one billion hours of video watched daily, and more than 500 hours of content uploaded per minute. YouTube generated $31.5 billion in advertising revenue in 2023, with combined ad and subscription revenue exceeding $50 billion from Q4 2023 to Q3 2024.

## Key Concepts

- **Founding origin**: Created by three ex-PayPal employees; originally conceived as a video dating site before pivoting to general video sharing
- **First video**: "Me at the zoo" uploaded April 23, 2005, by co-founder Jawed Karim
- **Acquisition**: Google purchased YouTube for $1.65B in Google stock (November 2006)
- **Scale**: ~14.8 billion total videos (mid-2024); 500+ hours uploaded per minute; accounts for ~16% of all internet traffic
- **Revenue model**: Primarily advertising via Google AdSense, supplemented by YouTube Premium subscriptions, channel memberships, and Super Chat donations
- **Product portfolio**: YouTube Kids, YouTube Music, YouTube Premium, YouTube Shorts, YouTube TV, Playables (gaming)
- **Content licensing**: Uploader retains copyright (standard license); Creative Commons optional
- **Tech stack**: Python (core/API), C (via CPython), C++, Java (via Guice), Go, JavaScript (UI)
- **Leadership timeline**: Founders (2005-2010) → Salar Kamangar (2010-2014) → Susan Wojcicki (2014-2023) → Neal Mohan (2023-present)
- **COPPA compliance**: Fined $170M by FTC for collecting minors' data; all "made for kids" videos treated as COPPA-liable since January 2020
- **Dislike count removal**: Public dislike counts hidden November 2021, citing cyberbullying concerns; controversial decision criticized by co-founder Karim
- **Ad blocker crackdown**: Began late 2023; server-side ad injection introduced 2024 to circumvent blockers
- **Age assurance**: Machine-learning-based age detection announced July 2025 for US trial, restricting features for detected minors

## Relationships

- **Google/Alphabet**: Parent company; YouTube uses Google AdSense for advertising and integrates with Google's broader ecosystem
- **Competing platforms**: TikTok and Instagram Reels (short-form via YouTube Shorts), Twitch (live streaming/gaming), Vimeo (creator-focused), Netflix (streaming)
- **Legal/regulatory**: Viacom v. YouTube (copyright, resolved 2012); COPPA/FTC enforcement; EU bandwidth reduction during COVID-19; UK Online Safety Act 2023; NO FAKES Act 2025 (AI-generated content)
- **Content ecosystem**: Vevo partnership (music videos); creator economy with monetization tools (AdSense, Super Chat, memberships, Premium revenue sharing)
- **Hardware/platform disputes**: Amazon Fire TV app removal (2018-19); Roku YouTube TV dispute (2020); Manifest V3 transition affecting browser extensions
