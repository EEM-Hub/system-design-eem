## Deadlock — Disambiguation Overview

This is a Wikipedia disambiguation page, not a technical deep-dive. It lists the many uses of the term "deadlock" across domains. For system design, the only relevant entry is **Deadlock (computer science)**: a situation where two or more processes are each waiting for the other to take action. The page itself contains no technical detail — it serves as a routing index.

## Key Concepts

- **Deadlock (computer science)**: Two or more processes each waiting for the other to release a resource, resulting in indefinite blocking. This is the definition relevant to system design.
- **Deadlock (game theory)**: A distinct mathematical concept where the mutually beneficial action is also the dominant strategy — not the same as the CS concept despite sharing a name.
- The term "deadlock" is overloaded across politics (gridlock), negotiation (impasse), law (hung jury), locksmithing (deadbolt), and sports (tie/draw).

## Relationships

- **Impasse / Gridlock**: General synonyms for deadlock in non-technical contexts; listed in "See also."

## Exam-Relevant Points

- **Do not confuse CS deadlock with game-theory deadlock.** In CS, deadlock is a failure state (processes stuck). In game theory, deadlock is a game type where cooperation emerges naturally from self-interest.
- The CS definition to memorize: **two or more processes each waiting for the other to take action**, resulting in none making progress.
