## Digital Wallets (E-Wallets / Mobile Wallets)

A digital wallet is an electronic device, online service, or software program that enables electronic transactions between parties. It can store payment methods, identity documents (driver's licenses, health cards, loyalty cards), and credentials, functioning both for online purchases and at physical points of sale via technologies like NFC.

## Key Concepts

- **Digital wallet** (also e-wallet, mobile wallet): software or device enabling electronic transactions by exchanging digital currency for goods and services
- **Client-side wallet**: stored on the user's device, self-maintained, compatible with most e-commerce sites
- **Server-side wallet** (thin wallet): created and maintained by an organization on its servers; gaining popularity due to security, efficiency, and user satisfaction benefits
- **Two components**: software component (security/encryption) and information component (database of user data — addresses, payment methods, etc.)
- **NFC (Near Field Communication)**: wireless protocol used to pass credentials from wallet to merchant terminal
- **Credential verification**: wallets increasingly authenticate holder identity beyond payments (e.g., age verification for alcohol purchases)
- **Cryptocurrency wallet**: a specialized digital wallet that stores private keys for cryptocurrencies
- **Financial inclusion**: 2025 research shows digital wallets lower access barriers and improve financial service availability for underserved populations

## Commands and Syntax

No CLI commands or configuration syntax apply — this is a conceptual/product domain topic rather than a technical implementation reference.

## Relationships

- **Mobile payments**: digital wallets are the primary enabler of mobile payment systems (M-PESA, Apple Pay, Google Wallet)
- **NFC / Bluetooth**: underlying communication technologies for contactless wallet transactions
- **E-commerce**: digital wallets auto-fill payment and shipping information across merchant sites
- **Cryptocurrency**: cryptocurrency wallets are a specialized subset storing private keys rather than fiat payment methods
- **Identity management**: digital wallets expanding from financial transactions into government ID storage (digital driver's licenses, GOV.UK wallet)
- **Payment service providers**: wallets interface with networks like MasterCard PayPass, iDEAL, and the European Payments Initiative (EPI)
- **Point of sale systems**: wallets interact with merchant terminals at physical retail locations

## Exam-Relevant Points

- A digital wallet has **two components**: software (encryption/security) and information (user data database)
- **Server-side (thin) wallets** are maintained by organizations on their servers; **client-side wallets** are stored on user devices
- **NFC** is the wireless technology for passing credentials to merchant terminals
- Digital wallets store more than payment info: driver's licenses, health cards, loyalty cards, and other ID documents
- **M-PESA** is a key example of mobile payment adoption in Kenya and Tanzania
- The **European Payments Initiative (EPI)** acquired iDEAL to create a pan-European digital wallet
- Security features include **biometric authentication** and **encryption**
- Digital wallets reduce fraud for merchants and provide faster payment receipt, decreased transaction costs, and decreased theft loss
- Asia is a leading region for adoption: 45% of users in China, 36.7% in India, 23.3% in Singapore (2015 MasterCard survey)
- Japan pioneered the concept with "wallet mobiles"
- US states have begun adapting digital driver's licenses for use at TSA checkpoints
